    Caste-wise Dropout Rates

  • Dropout Rates by Caste Categories in Different States of India

    • 1. Scheduled Caste (SC) Dropout Rate: - The highest SC dropout rate is in Bihar (17%), followed by Madhya Pradesh (15%). - The lowest SC dropout rate is in Tamil Nadu (9%), followed by West Bengal (11%).

    2. Scheduled Tribe (ST) Dropout Rate: - The highest ST dropout rate is in Bihar (16%), followed by Madhya Pradesh (14%). - The lowest ST dropout rate is in Tamil Nadu (8%), followed by West Bengal (10%).

    3. Other Backward Classes (OBC) Dropout Rate: - The highest OBC dropout rate is in Bihar (12%), followed by Uttar Pradesh (10%). - The lowest OBC dropout rate is in Tamil Nadu (5%), followed by Gujarat (6%).

    4. Open Category (OC) Dropout Rate: - The highest OC dropout rate is in Bihar (20%), followed by Madhya Pradesh and Andhra Pradesh (both 13%). - The lowest OC dropout rate in Tamil Nadu (4%), followed by Karnataka (6%).

    Economic Status-wise Dropout Rates

  • Analysis of Economic Status-wise Dropout Rates in Indian States

    • 1. Economically Disadvantaged Students: Bihar has the highest dropout rate among economically disadvantaged students, followed by Madhya Pradesh and Uttar Pradesh. Tamil Nadu has the lowest dropout rate among economically disadvantaged students, followed by Gujarat and Maharashtra.

    2. Economically Advantaged Students: Bihar also has the highest dropout rate among economically advantaged students, followed by Uttar Pradesh and Andhra Pradesh. Tamil Nadu has the lowest dropout rate among economically advantaged students, followed by Maharashtra and Gujarat.

    Overall, students from economically disadvantaged backgrounds tend to drop out of school more often than those from economically advantaged backgrounds. However, this varies from state to state. Factors like the quality of education, social support, and government policies also play a role in dropout rates.
